The lecture

The lecture gave an introduction to the course. We were shown how tinkering works, how it can be integrated and what the different "aspects" of tinkering can be. They were defined as the Design Goal, the Seed, the Toolbox and the Influence.

During the first half of the lecture we had to write down two tinkering experiences on post it notes. This can be seen in the figure below:

As you can see these aren't the largest tinkering projects, but they both really had a final goal in mind and arduinos here served as a medium to get there. Especially during the first one during my beachelors, I really felt like arduinos were a novel and scary thing that I could never get comfortable with. During the other mentioned tinkering project though I was already a lot more familiar with arduinos, really using them as the lecture defined as a "toolbox".

During the second half we had to come back to the tinkering projects and define the different aspects, in this case only Goal, Seed and Toolbox, for one of the ideas. I picked the one for the second year project.

I hold fond memories of this project as I think it turned out really great. We had to do some good designing on different aspects, such as faking a shredder, an elevator, and a gambling machine creation. While I was mainly on the programming team I got to help designing the different elements, mostly foccusing on the shredding element. As a non-create student this gave good knowledge of how the design process goes in the study, and I learned a lot!

At the end of the lecture we got homework to create a Building Block. This will be posted in a different page, which can be found in The Project Overview.
