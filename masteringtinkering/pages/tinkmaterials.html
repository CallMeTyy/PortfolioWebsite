Properties of Tinkering Materials


13-02-2026

During the second lecture/lab I, together with the other two people at my table, explored four different tinkering objects. We explored the totem maker kit, the minecraft maker kit, littlebits and micro:bits. The table below shows our findings. I also made some pictures along the way, which are shown below the table :)

Affordance: Self Guiding Affordance: Preciousness Affordance: Skills needed Affordance: Interoperable Novelty Low threshold, High ceiling Price
Totem maker kit Relatively self guiding , requires instructions to make more complex shapes. Not easy to imagine things what to do with it without instructions. Comes equipped with a saw to break it, it's not precious by design Requires precise motor skills and tool usage. Not suitable for kids, easy to use for adults Uses very standard things and can easily be connected to anything with holes Very similar to many tools, not a new idea but a good execution Easy to get into, gonna be very complex to make complicated things Expensive to get into, 100 euros for the starting kit. Relatively cheap to keep going, 10-30 euros for the actual things you use.
Minecraft maker kit Very hard to use, very large instruction book It's a breadboard with mostly programming related workings. The thing that does things is very precious and should not be broken. Requires a lot of programming skills, but might be easy to learn programming with if you already are motivated to play minecraft. Can make minecraft an input for other things as it connects to the raspberry pi At its time very cool and novel, now looks very dated with an old version of minecraft. Novel idea, outdated execution. High threshold to get into, probably also a high ceiling to master. Fairly expensive, 60 euros
Littlebits Needs a little introduction to learn what colors represent what part of the output, easy to use and learn afterwards Each part is very important and at the same time can be skipped. It's not something you'd fiddle with though, you leave it the way it comes out of the package You need very little skills to operate it, only need basic understanding of color and soundwaves Does not really work for other elements, might have some input ways that can connect with USB but did not try those. Not interoperable Very novel idea, very interesting way to learn and play with sound waves. Very easy to use and there's nothing like it I've seen before. Again, easy to get into. I assume when you play with it a lot more you might be able to get some really cool sounds. Expensive to very expensive. Starting kit is 80 euros, full kits are up to 400
Micro:bit Microbit has a website to easily start working with it, but you do need instructions to use it Very precious as it's just one piece, cannot really make modifications to it Requires a little experience with programming but can also be a very good tool to learn it. Very accessible The output/input contact pins can make it communicate with a variety of different other devices The simplicity is what makes it novel. Arduinos or raspis can have a similar experience, but the amount of feedback and simple sensors make it novel. Also the accessibility is new and novel. Very low threshold to get into, just open a website and hook it up to a pc. With different programming languages and the potential make the ceiling high too. Cheap, accessible. About 16 euros
