My Tinkering Spaces


Tinkering spaces

Tinkering is not something I often do physically. As mainly a programmer, most of what I would consider a tinkering project have been digital. I did however throughout the years do some physical tinkering as well, and these are the spaces I consider real tinkering spaces.

My current desk in Enschede where I do most of my tinkering.


If I run out of room, for example when making "Surprises", I move to the living room table. I consider this the place with the most potential. While mostly empty, it's a good space to both work at and get feedback from housemates while I am at it.


The design lab was a fun tinkering space during my pre-masters. I had a similar space during my baechelors, but I did not take pictures of this one. I spent many hours working on arduino elements here for the Create module 8 project or other prototyping tasks.


My electronic junk drawer with laptops, arduinos, random electronic parts and tools. I often like to just clean it up to see what kind of toys I can use when I have leftover time.

My thinking space

Besides just tinkering, thinking or thinkering is also a very important part in my opinion. These are the places where ideas are formed or grow. I have a very vivid imagination and often already start prototyping in my head before I even start putting things down on paper or start building.

The classic shower thought is not just for show. For some reason this place yields amazing results when in need of good ideas, or "seeds" for projects.


Another great spot for thinking is my bed. I like working in the evenings, and when things mentally block I find going to bed to be one of the best and worst ideas. While great for getting new ideas, this often comes at the cost of getting less sleep.


Finally just my usual chair in which I always sit. Just taking a step back, be it in a chair or going for a walk, really help with getting the old thinking machine back in shape. Walks are probably my favourite way of ideating, either alone or with a group of people.


Tinkering background

While tinkering is not neccesairly within my nature, I did grow up fascinated by electricity and making things. I loved legos and lego technic and you could often find me working on the little electric science kits such as the one in the photo.

These are the toys I ab-so-lutely adored as a child. There were many different pieces you could buy and I wanted all of them. I have fond memories of playing with these as a kid, and they are basically instructed tinkering kits.


And of course the legos with all the machines. While I did not have many of my own as a kid my best friend who had limited screen time and I would often play with these to make small cars, trains or other moving toys. I remember this cool cablecar I once made and man did I love cablecars ever since. At high school these were also readily available with a bunch of different sensors or actuators which was great. Proposed to do my "PWS" with them but sadly was not allowed.

My virtual tinkering space

See now this is my real space where I can use my "toolbox". I love prototyping and making little code snippets or simple interactions using Unity or other gameplay programming engines (e.g. processing). This is where I feel most at home tinkering.

Unity is my real "home". During my beachelors Creative Media and Game Technologies I really used this engine a lot, and I often find myself using it for the most simplest of project requests. While digital, this feels like tinkering the most to me as it really allows me to use my creativity to the max, realizing most things that come to mind without much effort.


When it comes to Arduino Tinkercad was my go-to program. While I like testing out projects in real life, having a place to simulate everything in a safe environment helped a lot for getting some confidence with using arduino. Great tool!


When it comes to ideating, the step before tinkering, paint is my best friend. Of course Photoshop is nice, but those simple quick ideas are always first visualized in paint.



After paint we of course move to photoshop or Goodnote on my ipad to do a proper scetch or wireframe. These tools I have often used and I feel really comfortable with.


3D is not my greatest skill, I wish to know it more. I have some limited experience with the modeler in Tinkercad and can use it to create simple representations of ideas I have for classes.


MagicaVoxel is a simple tool in which I can use "voxels", 3D pixels, to create different models. While it may seem out of place, this tool really is useful for making simple models when you cannot model. Its simplicity makes it easy to use and makes it easier to focus on creative freedom.


Verdict

That was my experience with Tinkering Spaces in a nutshell. While I do tinker from time to time, it's mainly digitally using Unity or other programs. I do like making things with my hands though, especially when it comes to things such as Surprises which I mentioned before. Creativity is something I often try to fuel with different aspects such as browsing art on social media or watching videos with cool projects to motivate me for new things to do.
