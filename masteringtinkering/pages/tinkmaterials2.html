Durability The parts are plastic and made to be shaped to size easily so they probably won't last forever under high use. They are however pretty thick in terms of plastic use so they could last a while. Overall sustainability The totem maker kit uses a lot of plastic which is not super sustainable. However, the parts can be taken apart and re-used for other projects. The littlebits are well-made and can withstand moderate use. However, the electronic components might degrade over time. The micro:bit is relatively sturdy and can handle typical usage scenarios without issue.

The micro:bit is compact and lightweight, making it easy to store and transport.
Cost (manufacturing) Very cheap to make and ship, probably also made in quite high quantities Hard to guess. Probably made in small batches but overall pretty cheap parts probably. Availability The totem maker kit is available from various online retailers and craft stores. The Minecraft maker kit may be harder to find and could be more expensive due to its niche market.
