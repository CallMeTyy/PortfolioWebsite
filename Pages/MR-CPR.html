Mixed Reality CPR Training


General Information

Summary: The MR CPR training was made in colaboration with SeriousXR as part of my thesis. It serves as a prototype in which several stressors were applied and evaluated on their effectivess together with CPR certified testers.
Coding Platform: Unity Engine
Target Platform: Standalone VR (Recommended for Quest 3)
Development Time: 20 Weeks (2024)
Developed under SeriousXR

Link to the paper: Public Thesis

Description & Contributions

This project was developed with the intention of doing research on a more psychological level and really research into how users interact with MR. I did all development in the project, and only relied on the use of commercially available free 3D models and animations. Sounds were also imported. I had no previous experience with AR before starting this project, nor did I ever use hand tracking. Everything was self-taught throughout my graduation period.

My Contributions
I worked on this project by myself. OpenXR was used in combination with XRHands and meta supported plugins to serve as the backbone of the project. These are the main focus points of development throughout the project:
- Desiging and prototyping several different stressors
- Creating an AI that can act as a bystander or a "sick" person.
- Hand tracking with gesture implementations
- AR aspects such as bringing real life furniture to a virtual environment
- Recreating the basics of CPR such as compressions that work on analogue dolls or pillows
- Menu selection, UI, multiple language support (Dutch and English), tutorial and other gameplay aspects
