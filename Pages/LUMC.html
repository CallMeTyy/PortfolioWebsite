LUMC Project


General Information

Summary: The LUMC project is a project that tries to create a training simulation for doctors training to perform an intubation. It's my internship project, and my introduction to Serious VR.
Coding Platform: Unreal Engine
Target Platform: PCVR
Development Time: 20 Weeks
Team Project at the Saxion XR Lab

Description & Contributions

During my time at the Saxion XR Lab I worked on various projects. During the LUMC project, my main project, I contributed to several "stages" of the intubation. The entire procedure of intubation could be divided into several "stages", and as we were the first engineer interns who worked on the project we started from stage 0, the preparation. The entire project was to be completed with multiple trainers, thus everything is networked synced.

My Contributions
I worked on the "Monitoring", the "Anesthesia" and the "Patient Placement" stages. During my time at the XR lab I mainly used blueprints in Unreal Engine to create the interactions as demonstrated in the videos below. The key points of this project are:
- Creating dynamic cables and placable stickers
- Recreating real life procedures in a digital environment
- Utilizing blueprints and a bit of C++ to create interactions in Unreal Engine
- Recreating real life machines in a digital environment and adding interactions
- Creating and altering animations for metahumans
- Creating interactions for a networking game
- Working in an existing team and joining an existing codebase

All projects at the Saxion XR Lab were not allowed to be shared, with exceptions for video showcases.
